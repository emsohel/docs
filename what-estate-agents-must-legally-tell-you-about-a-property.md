&nbsp;
<h3><span style="font-size: 12pt;"><strong>What Is Material Information (and Why It Matters)?</strong></span></h3>
&nbsp;

Material information is anything a typical buyer would need to know before making a decision to view, offer, or buy a property. That's not just our opinion--it's the legal definition provided by the <strong>National Trading Standards Estate and Letting Agency Team (NTSELAT).</strong>

&nbsp;

Under the law, we must include this information upfront--in the property listing, on portals, in brochures. No last-minute reveals. No surprises at contract stage. If it's relevant and we know it, we tell you.

&nbsp;
<h2><span style="font-size: 12pt;"><strong>Here's What We're Legally Required to Share in Property Listings</strong></span></h2>
&nbsp;
<h3><strong>? <span style="font-size: 12pt;">Category A: Must-Haves for Every Property</span></strong></h3>
&nbsp;
<ul>
 	<li><strong>Asking price</strong> - We're not fans of "POA" (Price on Application). You'll always see the actual price.</li>
 	<li><strong>Tenure</strong> - Whether it's freehold, leasehold, or shared ownership.</li>
 	<li><strong>Council Tax band</strong> - So you can get a feel for ongoing costs.</li>
</ul>
&nbsp;
<h3><strong>? <span style="font-size: 12pt;">Category B: When Relevant to the Property</span></strong></h3>
&nbsp;
<ul>
 	<li><strong>Property type and construction</strong> - Flat, detached, timber-framed, etc.</li>
 	<li><strong>Number, size, and layout of rooms</strong> - Backed by real measurements and a floorplan.</li>
 	<li><strong>Utilities</strong> - Gas or electric heating? Mains water or private supply?</li>
 	<li><strong>Broadband and mobile signal</strong> - Especially if it's limited.</li>
 	<li><strong>Parking</strong> - On-street? Driveway? Permit needed? We'll clarify.</li>
</ul>
&nbsp;
<h3><strong>? <span style="font-size: 12pt;">Category C: Specific to Certain Properties</span></strong></h3>
&nbsp;
<ul>
 	<li><strong>Building safety concerns</strong> - Including cladding, structural risks.</li>
 	<li><strong>Legal rights and restrictions</strong> - Listed buildings, conservation areas, public rights of way.</li>
 	<li><strong>Flood risk or coastal erosion</strong> - We disclose it when we know it.</li>
 	<li><strong>Planning permissions or local developments</strong> - If formal approval has been granted.</li>
 	<li><strong>Accessibility features</strong> - Like step-free access or adapted facilities.</li>
 	<li><strong>Coalfield or mining area status</strong> - Especially if it could impact your mortgage or insurance.</li>
</ul>

<h2><strong>? <span style="font-size: 12pt;">What We May Not Know (And Why That's Okay)</span></strong></h2>
&nbsp;

Sometimes, we're asked things like, "<strong>Is next door an Airbnb?</strong>" or "<strong>Are the neighbours nice?</strong>" And the truth is--we might not know.

&nbsp;

Why? Because unless the seller discloses it or it appears in public records, there's no legal obligation for that information to be provided. If it's not material information and it hasn't been shared with us, we won't have it.

&nbsp;

But let's be clear: not knowing something isn't the same as hiding something.

&nbsp;

For example, if the seller doesn't know their neighbour rents the property out as a holiday let, and we haven't been told, that's perfectly legal--and understandable. It's not something we're required to find out, and that's okay.

&nbsp;

That said, if you ask us a question and we don't know the answer--we'll always do our best to find it out. We're always happy to help.

<h2><strong><span style="font-size: 12pt;">FAQs</span></strong></h2>
&nbsp;

<strong>What if something isn't mentioned in the listing?</strong>

&nbsp;

Ask us! If we know it, we'll tell you. If it's legally required and missing, we'll chase it down. If it's not required and we don't have it, we'll explain why.

&nbsp;

<strong style="font-size: 16px;">Do you know what the neighbours are like?</strong>

&nbsp;

Unless there's a formal dispute disclosed in the TA6, probably not. And that's okay--it's private, and not part of our role. We are lucky that all our vendors have told us their neighbours are lovely but its down to personal opinion and not something we can comment on.

&nbsp;

<strong style="font-size: 16px;">Will you tell me if next door is an Airbnb?</strong>

&nbsp;

Not unless the seller has told us this information, and remember, the seller may not know themselves. It's not required material information and an AirBnB is just a short term rental property.

&nbsp;

<strong style="font-size: 16px;">Can I bring pets to a viewing?</strong>

&nbsp;

No. Please don't. Out of respect for the seller's home and preferences, animals aren't allowed at viewings

&nbsp;

<strong style="font-size: 16px;">Will you give me legal or structural advice?</strong>

&nbsp;

No, but we'll try our best to help you always and we'll point you in the right direction. That's your conveyancer's and surveyor's job. We'll provide all the information we can to support them.
